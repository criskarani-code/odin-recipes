Kenyan Ugali, Sukuma Wiki & Scrambled Eggs Recipe

Back to Odin Recipes Homepage

This is a classic Kenyan staple, often called the "Bachelor's Special." It is affordable, nutritious, and quick to make.

To prepare Kenyan ugali, sukuma wiki, and scrambled eggs, you will first cook the ugali in a pot, then the sukuma wiki in one pan, and finally the scrambled eggs in another.

The total preparation and cooking time for the meal is approximately 30 minutes.

Ingredients

For the Ugali:
  • 2 cups maize meal (white cornmeal)
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste
For the Sukuma Wiki:
  • 1 bunch collard greens (sukuma wiki), chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Optional: 2 cloves garlic, minced; pinch of cinnamon, ginger, or turmeric powder
For the Scrambled Eggs:
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 small onion, chopped

Instructions

  • Step 1: Prepare the Ugali
    1. Boil the water: In a large pot, bring 4 cups of salted water to a rolling boil over medium-high heat.
    2. Add cornmeal: Gradually add the maize meal, stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or spatula (locally called a mwiko) to avoid lumps.
    3. Cook the ugali: Reduce the heat to medium-low and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and forms a stiff paste or dough.
    4. Finish: Gather the ugali into a mound in the center of the pot and let it cook for an additional two minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  • Step 2: Cook the Sukuma Wiki
    1. Saut√© aromatics: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a separate pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and golden brown.
    2. Add tomatoes: Stir in the diced tomatoes and cook until they break down and soften, about 5 minutes. If using garlic or spices, add them now and cook for another minute.
    3. Cook greens: Add the chopped collard greens (sukuma wiki) and stir well to incorporate with the tomato mixture.
    4. Simmer: Cover the pan and let the greens steam and cook for about 5-6 minutes, or until tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then remove from heat and set aside.
  • Step 3: Scramble the Eggs
    1. Heat pan: In a third, smaller skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil or butter over medium heat.
    2. Scramble eggs: Beat the eggs in a small bowl with a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the beaten eggs into the hot skillet. As the edges set, gently pull the cooked egg towards the center with a spatula to create a fluffy texture.

      Cook until just set but still moist, as they will continue to cook off the heat.

  • Step 4: Serve
    1. Plate: Serve the hot ugali as a mound on a plate, with generous sides of the sukuma wiki and scrambled eggs.
    2. Garnish: Garnish with fresh lemon wedges for squeezing over the dish, if desired.

Serving

Place a portion of ugali on a plate, add a generous serving of sukuma wiki next to it, and the scrambled eggs on the side. This is traditionally eaten by hand by pinching a piece of ugali, making a small indentation, and using it to scoop up the greens and eggs.

Enjoy your Kenyan meal!
