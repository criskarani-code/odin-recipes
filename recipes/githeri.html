Kenyan Githeri Recipe

Back to Odin Recipes Homepage

Githeri is a simple, nutritious Kenyan one-pot meal consisting of boiled maize (corn) and beans, originating from the Kikuyu community.

While modern variations often include spices and vegetables, the traditional method focuses on the natural flavor of the maize and beans.

Here is a traditional, straightforward recipe for Githeri:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg Beans: Traditionally red kidney beans, rose cocoa, or pinto beans, pre-boiled until tender.
  • 5 Ears of Fresh Maize/Corn: Removed from the cob (or 2 cups dried maize, soaked overnight and pre-boiled).
  • 2 Onions: Finely chopped.
  • 2 Tomatoes: Chopped (optional, but commonly used for a little sauce).
  • 1 Tablespoon Oil or Fat: (e.g., vegetable oil).
  • Salt: To taste.

Instructions:

  1. If using dry ingredients, soak the beans and maize overnight.
  2. Boil the beans and maize together in a large pot with plenty of water until both are soft. (this can take 1–3 hours).
  3. Drain any excess water once cooked.
  4. Heat the oil in a large pot or sufuria.
  5. Add the chopped onions and sauté over medium heat until they are soft and translucent.
  6. Add the tomatoes and cook until they are soft and form a paste.
  7. Add the boiled maize and beans to the pot with the fried onions and tomatoes.
  8. Add salt, mixing well to ensure the flavors are distributed evenly.
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.

Serve:

Serve hot. Traditionally, it is accompanied by avocado or tea.

Enjoy your traditional Kenyan Githeri!
