Kenyan pilau Recipe

Back to Odin Recipes Homepage

Kenyan Pilau is an aromatic, one-pot spiced rice dish central to Swahili culture and a staple at celebrations.

The secret to an authentic dark brown pilau lies in caramelizing the onions—not the spices—until they are deep golden brown.

Ingredients:

  • Meat: 500g beef (sirloin or bone-in) or goat meat, cubed.
  • Rice: 2 cups long-grain Basmati or Pishori rice.
  • Aromatics: 2–3 large red onions (thinly sliced), 1 tbsp minced ginger, and 4–5 cloves of minced garlic.
  • Spices (Pilau Masala): You can use 2 tbsp of store-bought "Pilau Masala" or a whole spice mix including 1 tbsp cumin seeds, 1 cinnamon stick, 5–8 cardamom pods, 5–8 whole cloves, and 1 tsp black peppercorns.
  • Vegetables (Optional): 3 medium potatoes, peeled and quartered.
  • Liquid: 4 cups of beef broth (reserved from boiling the meat) or water.
  • Oil: ¼ cup vegetable oil or ghee.
  • Salt: To taste.

Preparation Steps:

  1. Boil the Meat: Place the meat in a pot with a little salt, garlic, and ginger. Cover with water and simmer until tender. Drain and reserve the stock (broth) for cooking the rice later.
  2. Caramelize Onions: In a large heavy-based pot (sufuria), heat oil over medium-high heat. Add sliced onions and fry for 10–15 minutes until they are a deep, dark brown. Do not burn them, as this will make the rice bitter.
  3. Add Aromatics & Spices: Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and your pilau masala spices (whole or ground). Sauté for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Brown the Meat & Potatoes: Add the pre-boiled meat and potatoes (if using). Stir well for 5 minutes so the meat absorbs the spice flavors and browns slightly.
  5. Simmer with Rice: Add the washed rice to the pot and stir for 2 minutes to coat it in the oil and spices. Pour in the reserved meat stock (standard ratio is 2 parts liquid to 1 part rice).
  6. Cook & Steam: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to the lowest setting. Cover tightly (using aluminum foil under the lid helps trap steam) and cook for 15–20 minutes until all liquid is absorbed.
  7. Rest & Serve: Turn off the heat and let the rice sit covered for 5–10 minutes. Fluff gently with a fork and serve with Kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad).
Enjoy your flavorful Kenyan Pilau!
